About Malaria

Malaria is a life-threatening infectious disease caused by Plasmodium parasites, primarily transmitted to humans through the bite of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. It presents with symptoms like fever, chills, sweats, fatigue, and can lead to severe complications if left untreated. Despite ongoing efforts to control and prevent the spread of malaria, it remains a significant global health challenge, particularly affecting people in tropical and subtropical regions, with a profound impact on public health and socioeconomic development.

Malaria Symtoms

Below are the some well known symptoms for malaria

  • extreme tiredness and fatigue 
  • impaired consciousness
  • multiple convulsions
  • difficulty breathing
  • dark or bloody urine
  • jaundice (yellowing of the eyes and skin) 
  • abnormal bleeding.
